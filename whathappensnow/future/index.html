future

what happens now

The greatest demand of LGBTQ+ youth mental health support is in the South, shown by the fact that they utilize social media resources the most in those states. However, LGBTQ+ youth suicide remains high in that area. Those areas have little protection against discrimination based on gender identity and sexual orientation, and most of them allow conversion therapy for minors. Despite attempts to try to use digital technology and social media as a way to provide outreach and support for the queer community, oppressive systems and cultures still dominate the area.

Social Media Utilization

How should we use social media?

  • Social media is not a means of therapy or mental health support for the LGBTQ+ youth community
  • The main purposes of social media seem to be creating a public image of an organization, promoting events, or raising funds
  • Overall, it's for supporting in-person resources for the community

Wellness for the LGBTQ+ Community

What needs to be addressed for LGBTQ+ youth wellness

  • We need a culture shift to create a space for LGBTQ+ youth to exist
  • Wellness for LGBTQ+ youth has specific requirements
  • More in-person centers for support are required

What does this all mean?

Suicide in the LGBTQ+ youth community is not the problem; it's a symptom of much larger issues in the United States that fail to protect queer people. Youths, who have the least amount of autonomy and power financially and legally, are at the greatest risk because systems of society treat them as if they're the other, and they don't have the ability to fight back.

For long-term wellness, we need extensive changes to our laws, our education and healthcare systems, and our culture as a whole. But until then, we need to provide in-person support for the children that need it most.
