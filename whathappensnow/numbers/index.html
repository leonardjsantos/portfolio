the current state of queer america

3

suicide is the third highest leading cause of death among young people between the ages of ten to twenty-four years old

lgbtq+ youths are at least four times as likely to commit and attempt suicide than their heterosexual, cisgender peers. this is likely to be a much larger number since this only accounts for those who openly identify as a part of the lgbtq+ community

4

8

culture shapes the way we personally interact with one another, and the acceptance and support of identity is crucial for young people. lgbtq+ youths are eight times as likely to commit suicide if they are rejected by the family upon coming out, as it removes any sense of personal foundation.

sixteen states in the united states do not offer any form of legal protection for housing, employment, and other rights against discrimination for lgbtq+ people. many of these states are where the highest rates of lgbtq+ youth suicide occur the most

35

the lgbtq+ community has historically been failed by the healthcare system in america. queerness was only removed as a mental illness by the american psychiatry association in 1973. however, claims that queerness is a condition to be cured continue to persist in our society. conversion therapy is completely legal in thirty-five of our fifty states in 2019.

the toll is too high. and it's likely to be higher.

according to a recent GLAAD study, twenty percent of youths younger than 24 years old identify as a part of the lgbtq+ community in some way

as the children of our nation are beginning to openly identify as lgbtq+, we must address the problems within our society that actively place them in danger of suicide.
